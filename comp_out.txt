The procedure for an elective bilateral vasectomy involves the following steps:

1. **Preparation**: The patient is placed in a supine position on the operating table. The scrotal area is shaved and prepped with a Betadine scrub to ensure a sterile environment.

2. **Anesthesia**: Local anesthesia is administered, typically using 2% Xylocaine or 0.25% Marcaine without epinephrine, to numb the scrotal skin.

3. **Incision and Exposure**: A small incision is made in the hemiscrotum (right side first, then left). The vas deferens is identified and grasped.

4. **Isolation and Removal**: A segment of the vas deferens (about 1–3 cm) is isolated, clipped proximally and distally, and then removed. The cut ends may be fulgurated (cauterized) to prevent any sperm passage.

5. **Closure**: Meticulous hemostasis is maintained, and the scrotal skin is closed with sutures (such as 4-0 chromic). 

6. **Post-Procedure Care**: The patient may be instructed to wear a jockstrap and apply a sterile dressing. They are also advised to use alternative contraception until confirming azoospermia with semen analyses.

Overall, the procedure usually results in minimal blood loss and no significant complications.
